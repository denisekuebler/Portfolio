Design einer Studie zur Konzeption einer bildungsbegleitenden Anwendung für Kinder mit Down-Syndrom

Project made in preparation for the Bachelor Thesis during the Summer-Semester 2019

Project Overview

  • Research on education, media usage and daily life of children with Down-Syndrome
  • The goal was to obtain knowledge which will help with conceptualising an E-Learning application for children with Down-Syndrome
  • Research about the target audience, including cognitive and physical abilities and restrictions
  • Design of a questionnaire targeted at the parents of children with Down-Syndrome
  • Design of an interview guide for personal interviews with the parents and teachers of the children
  • Conduction of Pre-Tests regarding the questionnaire and interview guides.

Research

  • Research on the domain "Down-Syndrome" including medical facts, cognitive and physical abilities and education.
  • Identifying questions that need to be answered through the study.
  • Substantiation of the necessity of digital learning for children with Down-Syndrome.
  • Research on possible data collection methods and identification of the best option in this situation.
  • Identification and choice of survey groups.

Designing the Questionnaire

  • Design of the Questionnaire based on the Book "Fragebogen - Ein Arbeitsbuch" by Rolf Porst.
  • Brainstorming and wording of questions.
  • Choosing questions out of the created question catalogue under the consideration of research ethics.
  • Choice of the question types and the order of questions.
  • Layouting and styling of the questionnaire.
  • Evaluating the questionnaire (understandability of the questions, size of the text fields, order of the question) with the help of Pre-Tests using testing interviewees.

Designing the Interview Guide

  • Brainstorming for questions asked in interviews.
  • Collecting questions in guides for interviews with parents and teachers of children with Down-Syndrome.
  • Layouting and styling was not necessary considering the guide would not be handed to the interviewees.
  • Pre-Testing was also not necessary because an interview has personal contact and presents the possibility for the interviewee to ask if questions are unclear.

Conducting the Survey

  • The questionnaire was sent to volunteering parents of children with Down-Syndrome either printed or online hosted on a website for conducting surveys.
  • Interviews were done with volunteers fitting the research group.
  • Results were collected and analysed in the bachelor thesis documented here
